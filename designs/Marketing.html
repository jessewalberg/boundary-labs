// section 03 · self-improving corpus

Every fail mutates the corpus. Every pass raises the bar.

Boundary Labs is closed-loop. When the target stops failing on a seed, the Red Team agents synthesize harder variants. When a class of attack lands, the corpus prioritizes it. The harness gets sharper with every run.

01

Seeds are drawn from a living corpus.

The corpus is versioned, tagged by category and severity, and weighted by how often the target survives them. Boring seeds get retired. Effective seeds spawn variants.

// 247 seeds · 11 generations · v.0.3.1
02

Probes traverse the authorized perimeter.

HTTP, SSE, or whatever surface you authorize. The harness mints synthetic sessions where the target requires authentication — never logs real secrets, never imports target modules.

// http · sse · smart launch · oauth
03

Judges verdict every response.

Semantic verdicts come from a judge model with a rubric. Deterministic verdicts check structural invariants. Verdicts disagree on purpose — every conflict surfaces in the console.

// pass · fail · partial · invalid
04

Failures mutate the seed corpus.

When a seed lands, the Mutator generates a family of harder variants. When the target now refuses all of them, those variants are sealed into the corpus and the cycle starts over.

// closed loop · weekly cadence

// section 07 · operating boundaries

What the harness will never do.

guarantee · 01

Synthetic data only.

No real PHI, PII, secrets, or production datasets. Synthetic seeds and synthetic patient contexts. Period.

guarantee · 02

Authorized targets only.

Targets are registered with explicit scope. The harness refuses to probe a domain that isn't on its allowlist.

guarantee · 03

No module import.

The runner does not import the target's source. The boundary between harness and target is process-level and audited.

guarantee · 04

Secrets are not logged.

Session secrets used to mint synthetic cookies live only inside the runner process. They never enter the run artifact.
